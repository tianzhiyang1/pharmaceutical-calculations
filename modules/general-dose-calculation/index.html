I. Term Definitions Review

+

Dose: The quantity of drug taken at one time. Can be single, daily, or total.

Dosage regimen: The schedule of doses (e.g., 1 tablet BID for 10 days).

Usual dose: The amount typically producing a therapeutic effect.

Loading dose: A large initial dose to achieve therapeutic levels rapidly.

Maintenance dose: A dose that maintains desired drug levels.

Prophylactic dose: A dose to prevent disease (e.g., antibiotics before dental procedures).

III. Dosing by Weight

+

In weight-based dosing, the medication dose is calculated based on the patient's body weight. It is typically expressed in milligrams (mg), micrograms (mcg), or grams (g) per kilogram (kg) of body weight.

FORMULA

Dose = Dose per weight × weight (kg)

Where:

  • Dose per weight is in mg/kg, mcg/kg, or g/kg
  • Weight is in kilograms (kg)
  • Note: If the patient's weight is given in pounds (lb), convert to kilograms first:

    Weight (lb)
    2.2

A patient is prescribed levothyroxine at a dose of 150 mcg/kg/day for severe hypothyroidism. The patient weighs 154 lb. How many milligrams of levothyroxine should the patient receive per day?

Step 1: Convert weight from lb to kg

Step 2: Convert dose units from mcg to mg

150 mcg = 0.15 mg (i.e., 0.15 mg/kg/day)

Step 3: Calculate the total daily dose by using the formula

Dose = Dose per weight × weight (kg)

= 0.15 mg/kg/day × 70 kg = 10.5 mg/day

IV. Fixed-Dose Combination Products

+

Fixed-dose combination products contain two or more active ingredients in a single dosage form (e.g., tablet or capsule). They are commonly used to:

  • Simplify medication regimens
  • Improve patient adherence
  • Reduce pill burden

While convenient, adjusting the dose of just one component in the combination is usually not possible without changing the dose of the other component(s). This may limit flexibility for dose adjustments based on patient needs.

Example Product:

A tablet labeled valsartan/hydrochlorothiazide 80 mg/12.5 mg contains:

  • 80 mg valsartan
  • 12.5 mg HCTZ

Example:

A patient is prescribed valsartan 160 mg and hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) 25 mg once daily. The pharmacy has available only the fixed-dose combination tablets of valsartan/hydrochlorothiazide 80 mg/12.5 mg. How many tablets should the patient take to receive the prescribed doses?

Step 1: Determine the dose of each component in one tablet

1 tablet = 80 mg valsartan + 12.5 mg HCTZ

Step 2: Calculate how many tablets are needed to reach the prescribed dose for each component

  • Prescribed valsartan dose: 160 mg
    Each tablet contains 80 mg valsartan, so:
    160 mg
    80 mg/tablet
    = 2 tablets
  • Prescribed HCTZ dose: 25 mg
    Each tablet contains 12.5 mg HCTZ, so:
    25 mg
    12.5 mg/tablet
    = 2 tablets

Step 3: Check that both component doses align with the number of tablets

2 tablets provide:

  • 2 × 80 mg = 160 mg valsartan
  • 2 × 12.5 mg = 25 mg HCTZ

So the answer: patient should take 2 tablets once daily

V. Tablet Splitting

+

Tablet splitting involves cutting tablets in half (or quarters) to:

  • Adjust doses when a lower strength is not available
  • Save cost (higher-strength tablets may be cheaper per mg)

Key Considerations:

  • Only split scored tablets approved by FDA
  • Do not split:
    • Extended-release
    • Delayed-release
    • Unscored tablets unless verified as safe
  • Splitting these forms can compromise drug release profiles and safety.

Example:

A patient is prescribed to take 0.3 mg colchicine daily using 0.6 mg scored tablets, for 30 days. How many colchicine tablets should be dispensed to the patient?

Step 1: Determine how the tablet can be split

The 0.6 mg colchicine tablet is a scored tablet that can be split.

Each 0.6 mg tablet can be split into 0.3 mg halves.

Step 2: Calculate the total number of doses needed

1 dose per day × 30 days = 30 doses

Step 3: Calculate the number of tablets needed

Each 0.6 mg tablet provides two 0.3 mg doses when split.

30 doses
2 doses/tablet
= 15 tablets

VI. Dose Range

+

Many pediatric medications are dosed based on body weight, with a recommended dose range (e.g., 60–75 mg/kg). This flexibility allows clinicians to adjust the dose according to the patient's clinical response and tolerance.

Example:

The usual dose of sulfisoxazole is 60–75 mg/kg per dose. A child weighs 44 lb. What is the range of appropriate doses?

Step 1: Convert weight from lb to kg

44 lb
2.2
= 20 kg

Step 2: Calculate the lower end of the dose range

60 mg/kg × 20 kg = 1200 mg

Step 3: Calculate the upper end of the dose range

75 mg/kg × 20 kg = 1500 mg

The appropriate dose range for this child is 1200 - 1500 mg per dose.
