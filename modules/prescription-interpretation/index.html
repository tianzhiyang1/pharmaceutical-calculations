Tutorial - Components of a prescription

  1. Prescriber information
  2. Patient information (name & address)
  3. Date prescription was written
  4. Superscription symbol: Rx

    Literally means "Take thou, or you take"

    This may or may not appear on the prescription

  5. Inscription: Medication (Name & strength)

    Also called the body of the prescription

  6. Subscription: Dispensing instructions to the pharmacist

    Usually includes quantity to be dispensed

    eg. amount of tablets, capsules, suppositories, grams, ml, fluidounces, etc.

    Examples:

    • No.
    • M ft tabs No. CXX
    • M ft ungt Disp 5 g
    • Disp caps No. xl (or XL)
    • Disp. tabs # C
  7. Signa or Sig: Directions to the patient

    "Signa" or "Sig" means "Mark thou."

    Prescriber indicates the directions for the patient's use of the medication

    Commonly are written using abbreviated forms of English or Latin terms,

    new terms made up by each prescriber, or a combination of each.

  8. Special instruction

    Include refill instructions, drug storage, etc

  9. Prescriber's signature and identification

    Include DEA # & license #

Tutorial - Number systems (Arabic and Roman)

ARABIC NUMERALS: A decimal system; Ten figures: zero and 9 digits

Rules:

  1. Different values are assigned to the digits according to the place they occupy in a row.

    Example: 123, 132, 213, 231, 321, 312

  2. The value of a digit is increased tenfold each time it moves one place to left

    Example: 9, 90, 900, 9000

  3. The value of a digit is decreased by one-tenth each time it moves one place to the right.

    Example: 50000, 5000, 500, 50

ROMAN NUMERALS: Positional notation

Rules:

  1. Roman numerals merely record quantities; they are of no use in computation
  2. Eight letters of fixed values used to express quantities in the Roman system
    Roman LettersValue
    ss1/2
    I or i1
    V or v5
    X or x10
    L or l50
    C or c100
    D or d500
    M or m1000

    Terminal i sometimes becomes a j

  3. When combining the symbols
    1. When the second letter is equal to or less than the first letter, their values are added

      Example: LX = 50 + 10 = 60

    2. When the second letter has a value greater than the first, the smaller is subtracted from the larger

      Example: XL = 50 - 10 = 40
