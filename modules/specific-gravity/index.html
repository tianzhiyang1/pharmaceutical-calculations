Tutorial - Density and specific gravity

I. Density

  • Definition:
    • Density =
      Mass
      Volume
    • Units: typically g/mL
  • Example:
    • If 10 mL of sulfuric acid weighs 18 g, density =
      18 g
      10 mL
      = 1.8 g/mL

II. Specific Gravity (SG)

  • Definition:
    • Specific gravity is the ratio of the weight of a substance to the weight of an equal volume of water.
  • Formula:
    • Specific gravity =
      weight of substance
      weight of equal volume of water
  • Since 1 mL of water weighs 1 g, for practical use:
    • Specific gravity =
      weight of substance (g)
      volume of substance (mL)
  • SG is unitless (e.g., alcohol SG = 0.81, glycerin SG = 1.25).

Common Uses of SG in Pharmacy

To convert weight (g) to volume (mL) or volume to weight, such as when compounding with liquids like syrup or alcohol.

Tip for the conversion between weight and volume using SG

  • Unit for weight is g; unit for volume is mL
  • Convert weight to volume (g → mL): volume (mL) =
    weight (g)
    SG
  • Convert volume to weight (mL → g): weight (g) = volume (mL) × SG

Example 1: Convert weight to volume

How many mL of a syrup (SG = 1.31) are needed to provide 100 g?

Convert using the formula:
volume =
weight
SG
=
100
1.31
= 76.3 mL

Example 2: Convert volume to weight

Convert 6.8 mL of methyl salicylate (SG = 1.174) to grams.

Convert using the formula:
weight = SG × Volume = 1.174 × 6.8 = 8 g

Tutorial - Enlarging and reducing formulas

I. Reducing or Enlarging a Formula

Adjusting a standard pharmaceutical formula to prepare a different final quantity while keeping the same proportions between ingredients.

II. Steps to Reduce or Enlarge a Formula

Use a conversion factor to scale every ingredient up or down proportionally.

Step 1: Determine the conversion factor

Conversion Factor =
Desired Quantity
Original Quantity

Step 2: Multiply each ingredient by the conversion factor

New amount = Original amount × Conversion Factor

Example 1 (Reducing a formula)

You are asked to compound 120 mL of a prescription based on a standard formula designed to prepare 500 mL of a mouthwash.

Original formula for 500 mL of mouthwash:

Ingredient Amount per 500 mL
Eucalyptol 2.5 mL
Menthol 1.0 g
Alcohol 95% 300 mL
Purified water qs 500 mL total
Step 1: Determine the conversion factor
Conversion factor =
120 mL
500 mL
= 0.24
Step 2: Multiply each ingredient by the conversion factor
Ingredient Original Amount Calculation New Amount
Eucalyptol 2.5 mL 2.5 × 0.24 0.6 mL
Menthol 1.0 g 1.0 × 0.24 0.24 g
Alcohol 95% 300 mL 300 × 0.24 72 mL
Purified water qs 500 mL Final volume should be 120 mL total qs ad 120 mL
Answer for example problem 1:
Ingredient Amount for 120 mL
Eucalyptol 0.6 mL
Menthol 0.24 g
Alcohol 95% 72 mL
Purified water qs 120 mL total
