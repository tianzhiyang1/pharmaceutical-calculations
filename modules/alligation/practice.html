Alligation Medial

+

Steps to solve medial alligation problems:

  1. Multiply each component’s volume/weight by its concentration to calculate the amount of active ingredient
  2. Add all the active ingredients to get the total active content
  3. Add all the volumes or weights to get the total quantity of the mixture
  4. Divide total active content by total quantity, then convert to % if needed

Alligation Alternate

+

Steps to solve alternate alligation problems:

  1. Identify known concentrations (high, low, and middle/desired)
  2. Set up the allegation scheme (alligation tic-tac-toe), plug in known values, and calculate parts of each component:
  3. Convert parts to actual quantity using proportion
