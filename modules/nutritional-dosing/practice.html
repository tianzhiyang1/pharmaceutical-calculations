BEE and TDE Calculations

+
M: BEE = 66 + (13.7 × W) + (5 × H) – (6.8 × A)
F: BEE = 655 + (9.6 × W) + (1.8 × H) – (4.7 × A)
TDE = BEE × activity factor × stress factor

Step 1: Gather patient information and convert units if necessary

  • Age (in years)
  • Weight (in kg)
  • Height (in cm)
  • Sex (male or female)

Step 2: Calculate BEE using the Harris-Benedict equation

  • Use the appropriate formula based on sex
  • W = weight (kg), H = height (cm), A = age (years)
  • BEE is expressed in kcal/day

Step 3: Apply activity and stress factors if needed

  • Activity factors: Confined to bed (1.2), Ambulatory (1.3)
  • Stress factors: Non-stressed (1.0), Surgery (1.2), Infection (1.4–1.6), Trauma (1.3–1.5), Burns (1.5–2.1)

Caloric Conversion Questions

Step 1: Know the caloric conversion values

  • Carbohydrates: 4 kcal/g (enteral), 3.4 kcal/g (parenteral)
  • Proteins: 4 kcal/g (both enteral and parenteral)
  • Fats: 9 kcal/g (enteral), 10%: 1.1 kcal/mL, 20%: 2 kcal/mL, 30%: 3 kcal/mL (parenteral)
