Tutorial - Percentage Concentration

Three Types of Percentage Strengths

Type Formula Application
Weight-in-volume (w/v) grams/100 mL Used for solids dissolved in liquids; Expresses the number of grams (g) of a substance in 100 mL of a solution or liquid prep.
Volume-in-volume (v/v) mL/100 mL Used for liquids in liquids; Expresses the number of mL of a substance dissolved in 100 mL of a solution or liquid prep.
Weight-in-weight (w/w) grams/100 g Used for solids in solids or heavy liquids; Expresses the number of grams of a substance in 100 grams of solid or liquid prep.
IMPORTANT TO REMEMBER
% means: x parts per 100 parts
w/v (weight in volume): g per 100 mL
v/v (volume in volume): mL per 100 mL
w/w (weight in weight): g per 100 g
Matches: 0/0

Converting Between Decimal and Percentage

To convert percentage to decimal: Drop the % sign and divide by 100

See the examples below:

10%
Drop % sign
10
Divide by 100 (move decimal 2 places left)
10 / 100 = 0.10
Result: 0.1
0.9%
Drop % sign
0.9
Divide by 100 (move decimal 2 places left)
0.9 / 100 = 0.009
Result: 0.009

To convert decimal to percentage: Multiply by 100 and add the % sign

See the examples below:

0.1
Multiply by 100 (move decimal 2 places right)
0.1 × 100 = 10.0
Add % sign
10%
Result: 10%
0.009
Multiply by 100 (move decimal 2 places right)
0.009 × 100 = 0.9
Add % sign
0.9%
Result: 0.9%

Example 1:

What is the percentage strength (w/v) of a solution if 4 fl oz contains 6 g of a drug?

Step 1: Identify the type of percentage

The type of percentage is weight in volume (w/v).

Step 2: Convert all units to standard units

We need to convert fluid ounces to mL:

4 fl oz × 30 mL/fl oz = 120 mL

The weight is already in grams (6 g), so no conversion needed.

Step 3: Set up the proportion

Set up the ratio and proportion using the format:

Amount of drug
Total amount
=
x
100
6 g
120 mL
=
x g
100 mL
Step 4: Solve for x
  1. Cross-multiply and solve the equation
6 g × 100 mL = 120 mL × x g
600 = 120 × x
x = 600 ÷ 120 = 5

Therefore, the percentage strength is 5% (w/v).

Example 2:

How many milliliters of alcohol are needed to prepare 1 quart of a 70% (v/v) alcohol solution?

Step 1: Identify the type of percentage:

v/v

Step 2: Convert units:

1 quart = 946 mL

Step 3: Set up the proportion:

x mL alcohol
946 mL solution
=
70 mL alcohol
100 mL solution

Step 4: Solve for x:

x = (70 × 946) ÷ 100 = 662.2 mL

Therefore, 662 mL of alcohol is needed (rounded to the nearest whole number).

Example 3:

A pharmacist needs to make 10% (w/w) zinc oxide ointment using 5 g of zinc oxide. How much total ointment can be prepared, and how many grams of vaseline base are required?

Step 1: Identify the type of percentage:

w/w

Step 2: Convert units:

Units are already in grams, so no conversion is needed.

Step 3: Set up the proportion to find the total ointment weight:

5 g zinc oxide
x g total ointment
=
10 g zinc oxide
100 g total ointment

Step 4: Solve for x. First find the total ointment weight:

x = (5 × 100) ÷ 10 = 50 g total ointment

Calculate vaseline base needed:

Vaseline base = 50 g - 5 g = 45 g

Tutorial - Ratio Strength

Ratio strength expresses the concentration of an agent relative to the total preparation in the form of a ratio.

  • The concentrations of weak solutions frequently are expressed as ratio strength (usually, 1 part in some # of parts).
  • Since percentage strength are ratios of parts per 100, ratio strength is another way of expressing percentage strength.
IMPORTANT TO REMEMBER

1:1000 is interpreted as:

For solid in liquid: 1 gram in 1000 mL
For liquid in liquid: 1 mL in 1000 mL
For solid in solid: 1 gram in 1000 grams
Match the condition to its interpretation!
Matches: 0/0

Special Case: Parts Per Million (ppm)

Used for very diluted preparations and set limits

e.g., "not more than 6 ppm" means 6/1,000,000

Example 1:

Express 0.025% (w/v) as a ratio strength

Step 1: Identify and interpret information

0.025% (w/v) means: 0.025 g / 100 mL

Step 2: Set up the proportion to find the ratio strength
0.025 g
100 mL
=
1 g
x mL
Step 3: Solve for x
x = 4000 mL

Therefore, the ratio strength is 1:4000 (w/v)

Example 2:

Express 1:5000 (w/v) as a percentage

Identify and interpret information: 1:5000 (w/v) means: 1 g / 5000 mL.

Set up the proportion to find the percentage:

1 g
5000 mL
=
x g
100 mL

Solve for x:

x = (1 × 100) ÷ 5000 = 0.02

Therefore, 1:5000 (w/v) = 0.02% (w/v)

Example 3:

If there are 100 mg of sodium chloride in 1 liter of solution, calculate the ratio strength (w/v) of the solution.

Identify the type of ratio strength: w/v.

Convert all units to standard units:

  • 100 mg = 0.1 g
  • 1 L = 1000 mL

Set up the proportion:

0.1 g
1000 mL
=
1 g
x mL

Solve for x:

x = (1 × 1000) ÷ 0.1 = 10,000

Therefore, the ratio strength is 1:10,000 (w/v)

Example 4:

A preservative is present at a concentration of 5 ppm in a 250 mL ophthalmic solution. How many milligrams of the preservative are in the solution?

Identify the type of ratio strength: w/v.

Convert units and understand ppm:

  • 5 ppm = 5 parts per 1,000,000 parts = 5 g / 1,000,000 mL

Set up the proportion:

5 g
1,000,000 mL
=
x g
250 mL

Solve for x:

x = (5 × 250) ÷ 1,000,000 = 0.00125 g = 1.25 mg

Therefore, the solution contains 1.25 mg of preservative
