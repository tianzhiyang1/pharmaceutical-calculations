E-Value Formula

+
E
=
58.5 × i
MW × 1.8

Where:

  • E = sodium chloride equivalent (E value)
  • 58.5 = molecular weight of NaCl
  • i = dissociation factor of the drug
  • MW = molecular weight of the drug
  • 1.8 = dissociation factor of NaCl

Sodium Chloride Equivalent Method

+

Steps for the Sodium Chloride Equivalent Method:

  1. Calculate the amount of NaCl required to make the entire solution isotonic:
    • Use proportion: 0.9 g NaCl / 100 mL = x g NaCl / Total volume (mL)
  2. Calculate the amount of NaCl represented by the drug:
    • NaCl equivalent = Drug amount (g) × E value of the drug
  3. Subtract Step 2 from Step 1:
    • NaCl to add = Step 1 NaCl − Step 2 NaCl
  4. For isotonic adjustment with other agents (e.g., boric acid):
    • Convert NaCl amount from step 3 to the amount of the other agent
    • Amount of agent = Step 3 NaCl to add ÷ E value of the other agent
