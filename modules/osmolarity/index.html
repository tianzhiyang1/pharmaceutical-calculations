Tutorial - Osmolarity and Osmole

I. Concept review:

+

Osmole or milliosmole: A unit that used to measure the osmotic activity of a substance.

Based on: The total number of cations and total number of anions (total numbers of PARTICLES).

Osmolarity: An expression of osmotic concentration as osmoles per liter or milliosmoles per liter (mOsm/L).

Important: Osmolarity of a solution is the SUM of the osmolarities of the SOLUTE components of the solution.

Clinical Application: The labels of Pharmacopeial solutions that provide iv replenishment of fluid, nutrients, or electrolytes, and the osmotic diuretic mannitol are required to state the osmolar concentration.

Osmolarity vs. Osmolality

  • Osmolarity – measure of osmoles of solute/L of solution
  • Osmolality – measure of osmoles of solute/kg of solution

For very diluted aqueous solution, osmolarity and osmolality are almost identical.

II. Osmolarity calculation

KEY FORMULA
mOsm/L =
weight (g/L) × no. of species × 1000
MW

Where:

  • Weight (g/L) refers to the concentration in grams per liter.
  • Number of species (also called dissociation particles):
    • Osmotic pressure is proportional to the total number of particles in solution
    • A nonelectrolyte (e.g., Dextrose) → no dissociation → number of species = 1
    • An electrolyte (e.g., NaCl, KCl) → dissociation; e.g., NaCl – dissociate into one sodium ion and one chloride ion → number of species = 2

Species of dissociating molecules:

Compound Dissociation Species
NaCl Na⁺ + Cl⁻ 2
KCl K⁺ + Cl⁻ 2
NH₄Cl NH₄⁺ + Cl⁻ 2
NaHCO₃ Na⁺ + HCO₃⁻ 2
KC₆H₁₁O₇ K⁺ + C₆H₁₁O₇⁻ 2
MgSO₄ Mg²⁺ + SO₄²⁻ 2
FeSO₄ Fe²⁺ + SO₄²⁻ 2
CaCl₂ Ca²⁺ + 2Cl⁻ 3

Species of non-dissociating molecules: e.g., dextrose, mannitol; species = 1

III. Need to differentiate Osmolarity vs. Osmole

Osmolarity/Milliosmolarity: Is concentration; expressed Osmoles/L or Milliosmoles/L; will always need to be normalized to a volume of 1 liter

Osmole/Milliosmole: Is quantity; Osmole/Milliosmole calculations do not need to be normalized to 1 liter

Steps for calculating mOsmolarity (mOsm/L)

Step 1: Write the formula

mOsm/L =
weight (g/L) × no. of species × 1000
MW

Step 2: Convert the weight to grams per liter (g/L)

Step 3: Determine the number of species (particles):

  • Nonelectrolytes (e.g., dextrose, mannitol) do not dissociate → use 1
  • Electrolytes dissociate → count total ions

Step 4: Plug values into the formula and calculate mOsm/L

Example 1:

A solution contains 5% of anhydrous dextrose in water for injection. How many milliosmoles per liter are represented by this concentration? (dextrose MW = 180). Round the final answer to the nearest whole number.

Step 1: Write the formula
mOsm/L =
weight (g/L) × no. of species × 1000
MW
Step 2: Convert the weight to grams per liter (g/L)

5% → 5 g/100 mL = 50 g / 1000 mL = 50 g/L

Step 3: Determine the number of species (particles)

Dextrose (nonelectrolyte) → no. of species = 1

Step 4: Plug values into the formula and calculate mOsm/L
mOsm/L =
50 × 1 × 1000
180
= 278 mOsm/L
