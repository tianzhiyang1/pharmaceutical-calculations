Tutorial - Systems of Weights and Measures

International System of Units (SI)

A decimal system of weights and measures also called metric system - internationally recognized

Primary units of the metric system are:

  • Length - meter
  • Volume - liter
  • Weight - gram

Denomination Reduction

Restatement of a given quantity in terms of a higher OR lower denomination - by moving decimal point.

Examples:

  • Converting grams to milligrams: Move decimal point 3 places to the right
0.025 g
Decimal moves 3 places right
25.0 mg
  • Converting liters to milliliters: Move decimal point 3 places to the right
3.8 L
Decimal moves 3 places right
3800.0 mL

Common Systems

Apothecaries' System

Traditional system of pharmacy although it has been largely replaced.

Apothecaries' Fluid Measure:

From To Symbol

8 fluidrams

Apothecaries' Weight Measure:

Derived from 1 grain = weight of one grain of wheat

From To Symbol

Avoirdupois System

Common system of commerce, used in the US.

Avoirdupois Weight Measure:

From To Symbol

16 ounces
(7000 grains)		 1 pound oz → lb

Note: The Avoirdupois ounce (437.5 grains) differs from the Apothecaries' ounce (480 grains)
