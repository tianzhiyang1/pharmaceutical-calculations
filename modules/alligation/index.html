Tutorial - Alligation

Alligation: A method of solving problems that involves the mixing of solutions or mixtures of solids possessing different percentage strengths.

There are two types of alligation methods:

  • Alligation medial: Used when given multiple concentrations and quantities, and asked to find the final concentration of the mixture.
  • Alligation alternate: Used when mixing two concentrations to obtain a third (middle) concentration. It helps find the ratio or quantity of each component.

Alligation Medial

Used when given multiple concentrations and quantities, and asked to find the final concentration of the mixture.

Steps:

  1. Multiply each component's volume/weight by its concentration to calculate the amount of active ingredient.
  2. Add all the active ingredients to get the total active content.
  3. Add all the volumes or weights to get the total quantity of the mixture.
  4. Divide total active content by total quantity, then convert to % if needed.

Example 1: Alligation Medial

What is the percentage strength of sucrose in a mixture of 500 mL of an aqueous solution containing 40% w/v sucrose, 400 mL of a second aqueous solution containing 21% w/v sucrose, and 100 mL of purified water to make a total of 1000 mL?
