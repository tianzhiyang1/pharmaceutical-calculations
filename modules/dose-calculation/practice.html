Body Weights (BMI, IBW, AdjBW)

+
BMI =
Weight (kg)
[Height (m)]²
OR BMI =
Weight (lbs) × 703
[Height (inches)]²
IBW for males = 50 + 2.3 × (inches over 5 ft)
IBW for females = 45.5 + 2.3 × (inches over 5 ft)
AdjBW0.4 = IBW + 0.4 × (TBW - IBW)

Choosing the Right Weight for Drug Dosing:

  1. Compare TBW and IBW.
  2. If TBW is close to IBW (normal or underweight), use TBW for most medications.
  3. If the patient is obese (TBW > 120% of IBW), use:
    • IBW for drugs such as aminophylline, acyclovir, and levothyroxine.
    • AdjBW for aminoglycosides (e.g., tobramycin).
    • TBW for drugs like vancomycin and heparin.
  4. Follow institutional policy, exam instructions, and current clinical guidelines.

CrCl Dosing

+

Cockcroft & Gault Equation:

Male: CrCl (mL/min) =
(140 – Age in yrs) × (BW in kg)
72 × (SCr in mg/dL)
Female: CrCl (mL/min) = CrCl (male) × 0.85

Additional notes on CrCl estimation (institutional variability):

  • Low serum creatinine in elderly or cachectic patients:
    For older adults (≥65 years) or cachectic individuals (actual body weight ≥20% below IBW), it is sometimes recommended to round serum creatinine (SCr) up to 1.0 mg/dL. This adjustment helps avoid overestimating renal function due to falsely low SCr.
  • Short stature adjustments:
    For patients under 5 feet tall, standard IBW formulas may overestimate ideal weight.
    • For individuals under 4′10″: some protocols recommend capping IBW at 45.5 kg for males and 40.9 kg for females.
    • The Schwartz equation may be considered for more accurate estimation in pediatric population.
  • Weight selection for CrCl calculation:
    Refer to institutional policies to determine whether to use TBW, IBW, or AdjBW in the Cockcroft-Gault equation. Different institutions may have specific policies based on BMI, renal dosing guidelines, or drug-specific recommendations.

BSA Dosing

+

Mosteller Equation:

BSA (m²) =
Weight (kg) × Height (cm)
3600
OR    BSA (m²) =
Weight (lbs) × Height (in)
3131

Clinical Applications:

  • BSA is primarily used for chemotherapy and pediatric dosing.
  • Provides more accurate dosing than weight alone for certain medications.
  • Commonly used in oncology protocols and pediatric medicine.

Step-by-step calculation:

  1. Convert weight to kg and height to cm (if needed)
  2. Apply the Mosteller equation to calculate BSA
  3. Use BSA in dose calculation: Dose = mg/m² × BSA
